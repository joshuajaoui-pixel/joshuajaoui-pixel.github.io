🔬 Cell Biology

Animal cells: nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria, ribosomes.

Plant cells: all of the above PLUS cell wall (cellulose), large vacuole, chloroplasts.

Bacterial cells (prokaryotic): no membrane-bound nucleus. Have: cell wall, cell membrane, cytoplasm, ribosomes, plasmids, chromosomal DNA free in cytoplasm.

Specialised cells

  • Red blood cells: biconcave disc (↑ surface area), no nucleus (more haemoglobin), flexible to squeeze through capillaries
  • Sperm cells: tail for movement, many mitochondria (energy), acrosome (enzymes to penetrate egg)
  • Nerve cells: long axon (fast signals), myelin sheath (insulation), many dendrites
  • Root hair cells: long extension (↑ surface area for absorption), no chloroplasts (underground)

Magnification

Magnification = Image size ÷ Actual sizeLight microscope: up to ×2000. Electron microscope: up to ×2,000,000 — far higher resolution but kills cells.
🌿 Photosynthesis & Respiration
6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂Photosynthesis — needs light & chlorophyll. In chloroplasts.
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATPAerobic respiration — in mitochondria. Lots of energy.
Glucose → Lactic acid + small ATPAnaerobic (animals) — builds up, causes cramp.
Glucose → Ethanol + CO₂ + small ATPAnaerobic (yeast/plants) — fermentation.

Limiting factors of photosynthesis

  • Light intensity: more light → faster rate (up to a point). At night = 0.
  • CO₂ concentration: more CO₂ → faster (up to a point).
  • Temperature: optimum ~25–35°C. Too hot → enzymes denature. Too cold → enzymes slow.
🧬 Genetics & Inheritance

DNA is a double helix. A pairs with T; C pairs with G. A gene codes for a specific protein.

  • Allele: a version of a gene. Dominant (capital) always shown; recessive (lowercase) needs two copies.
  • Homozygous: two identical alleles (AA or aa). Heterozygous: two different alleles (Aa).
  • Mitosis: 2 identical diploid cells — growth & repair.
  • Meiosis: 4 non-identical haploid cells — sex cells (gametes).
⚠️ Punnett square Aa × Aa → 25% AA : 50% Aa : 25% aa. Probability of recessive phenotype = 25%.
🦠 Disease & Immunity
  • Bacteria: prokaryote, produce toxins, treated with antibiotics. e.g. Salmonella, TB.
  • Viruses: not cells, hijack host DNA, no cure (antivirals only). e.g. Influenza, HIV.
  • Fungi: e.g. athlete's foot. Protists: e.g. malaria (mosquito vector).
  • Phagocytes: engulf & destroy pathogens. Lymphocytes: produce specific antibodies.
  • Memory cells remain after infection → faster response on re-exposure = immunity.
  • Vaccines: introduce weakened/dead pathogen → antibody production + memory cells without disease.
🌍 Evolution & Ecosystems

Natural selection (Darwin): (1) Variation exists. (2) Better-adapted individuals survive & reproduce more. (3) Traits passed to offspring. (4) Over generations → evolution.

Food chains: Producer → Primary consumer → Secondary → Tertiary. ~10% energy transferred at each level.

⚠️ Evolution acts on POPULATIONS over generations — individual organisms don't evolve.

☢️ Radioactivity & Space
  • Alpha (α): 2p+2n. Stopped by paper. Most ionising. Atomic number −2, mass −4.
  • Beta (β): fast electron. Stopped by aluminium. Atomic number +1.
  • Gamma (γ): EM wave. Stopped by lead. Least ionising, most penetrating.
  • Half-life: time for half the nuclei to decay. After 3 half-lives → ⅛ remains.

Big Bang evidence: redshift (galaxies moving away) & cosmic microwave background radiation.

⚛️ Atomic Structure & Periodic Table

Proton: +1, mass 1. Neutron: 0, mass 1. Electron: −1, negligible mass. Atomic number=protons. Mass number=protons+neutrons.

  • Group number = outer electrons. Period number = electron shells.
  • Group 1: reactivity INCREASES down (outer electron further from nucleus, more shielded).
  • Group 7: reactivity DECREASES down. More reactive halogen displaces less reactive one.
  • Transition metals: high density/MP, form coloured compounds, good catalysts.
🔗 Bonding
  • Ionic: metal+non-metal. Transfer of electrons. Giant lattice. High MP. Conducts when dissolved/molten.
  • Covalent: non-metal+non-metal. Sharing electrons. Simple molecular (low MP) or giant covalent (very high MP).
  • Metallic: positive ions in sea of delocalised electrons. High MP, conducts, malleable.
  • Graphite: giant covalent BUT has delocalised electrons → conducts. Layers slide → lubricant.
⚗️ Rates, Energy & Reactions
  • Rate increases with: higher temp · higher concentration · smaller particles · catalyst.
  • Exothermic: releases heat (combustion, neutralisation, respiration). ΔH negative.
  • Endothermic: absorbs heat (photosynthesis, thermal decomposition). ΔH positive.
  • Acid + metal carbonate → salt + water + CO₂. Acid + metal → salt + H₂.
⚠️ OIL RIG: Oxidation Is Loss of electrons. Reduction Is Gain.
🛢️ Organic Chemistry & Electrolysis
Alkanes: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂Saturated (single bonds only). e.g. methane CH₄.
Alkenes: CₙH₂ₙUnsaturated (C=C double bond). Decolourises bromine water.
  • Cracking: breaks long alkanes → shorter alkanes + alkenes. Thermal (high temp) or catalytic (zeolite).
  • Electrolysis of brine: cathode→H₂, anode→Cl₂, NaOH left in solution.
  • Cathode (−): reduction (cations gain electrons). Anode (+): oxidation (anions lose electrons).
